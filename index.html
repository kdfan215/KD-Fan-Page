Bio

Kevin Wayne Durant is a professional basketball player who currently plays for the Brooklyn Nets. He was born September 29, 1988 in Washington D.C. Durant was raised by his mother Wanda and Grandmother, Barbara. He has a sister Brianna and two brothers, Tony and Rayvonne.

He played one year of college basketball for the Texas Longhorns. He decided to wear the number 35 in honor of his former coach, Charles Craig. For his outstanding play, Durant was recognized as the unanimous national player of the year, winning the John R. Wooden Award, the Naismith College Player of the Year Award, and all eight other widely recognized honors and awards.

After one year with the longhorns, Durant decided to declare for the 2007 NBA draft at 18 years old He was drafted by the Seattle Supersonics as the second overall pick. Durant was an instant star in the NBA and won Rookie of the Year with impressive statistics of 20.3 ppg, 4.4 rpg and 2.4 apg. He joined Lebron James and Carmelo Anthony as the only teenagers to average at least 20 points per game.

After Durant's rookie year, the Seattle Supersonics reloacated and became the Oklahoma City Thunder. Durant's breakout year was 2010, his 3rd year in the NBA. He averaged a spectacular 30.1 ppg, becoming the youngest player in league history to win the Scoring Title. He would go on to win 2 more in a row. He could've won 4 in a row but he unselfishly decided to play more team ball, giving Carmelo Anthony of the New York Knicks the chance to steal the Scoring Title away from him in 2013. In 2012 Durant led his team to the NBA finals at only 23 years old. While he and his all-star teammate Russell Westbrook played phenominally in the finals, a combination of horrible officiating and unreliable play from teammates (James Harden) would keep Kevin from winning the 2012 NBA Championship. In 2013, Durant and the Thunder were primed for another deep playoff run. Patrick Beverly of the Houston Rockets had other plans. He decided to run into Russell Westbrook's knee after a time out was called. This tore Russell's meniscus rendering him injured for the rest of the year. While Durant was able to carry his team through the Rocket's series, he did not have enough manpower to beat the Memphis Grizzlies in the second round. Durant won his first league MVP award the following year averaging 32 ppg, 8 rpg and 5 apg. Durant made it to the Western Conference finals against the San Antoinio Spurs. They were favored to win the series until Durant's all defensive teammate, Serge Ibaka got injured for 2 games. They lost those 2 games but won the next two with Ibaka back, who was not at full strength. They would lose games 5 and 6 ending Durant's season. The following year was a tough one for Durant. He broke his foot about halfway through the 2014-2015 season and was out for six months. The following year Durant neary led his team to the 2016 Finals. He had a 3 games to 1 game advantage in the Western Conference Finals against the Golden State Warriors. The Warriors would come back to win the next 3 games and advance to the finals. Ultimately the Warriors would blow a 3-1 lead in the finals to the Cleveland Cavaliers.

After the 2016 season, Durant announced he would join the Golden State Warriors. Many looked upon this as an unfavorable move because Golden State had the greatest regular season record in NBA history the previous year. Durant faced a lot of ridicule and criticism from analysts and players alike. That did not stop him from continuing his amazing career. Durant led the Warriors to the 2017 NBA Championship and was named Finals MVP upon winning the title. He would go on to repeat this in 2018. The Warriors were expected to threepeat in 2019, but Durant was injured in the second round of the playoffs after dominating against the Clippers in the first round. He would not play until game 5 of the NBA Finals. With the Warriors down 3-1 against the Raptors, Durant's return would offer hope of a possible comeback to win the Championship. In game 5 Durant was terrific in the first quarter but tore his achilles early in the second quarter. The Toronto fans inexlicably cheered when Durant got injured, prompting looks of disgust from both Golden State and Toronto players. Toronto point guard Kyle Lowry was so appalled he even signaled his fans to be quiet by shushing them. The fans eventually did. But it was too late. They are considered some of the most classless NBA fans. Thanks in part to Durant's 11 points, the Warriors would win this game by 1 point. Without Durant, the Warriors would go on to lose game 6, making the Toronto Raptors the new NBA champs. In July 2019, Durant announced he would be joining the Brooklyn Nets on a 4 year contract. Though he will most likely injured his entire first year, many medical specialists believe he will come back and be as good as ever. If anyone can it is him.

KD Charity Foundation

The mission of the Kevin Durant Charity Foundation (KDCF) is to enrich the lives of at-risk youth from low-income backgrounds through educational, athletic and social programs.

In 2018, KDCF made a $10M commitment to bring College Track and The Durant Center to Prince George's County, pledged $3M to support University of Texas Basketball and the Center of Sports Leadership and Innovation, and completed five basketball court renovations around the world.
